Welcome

To the dedicated professionals of HHS,

At the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, we carry a clear responsibility: restore trust in public health. That requires transparency. It demands accountability. It puts people first.

Across the country, I hear the same message. Families want honest science. Patients want informed choices. Communities want solutions that deliver results.

This weekly newsletter will show you exactly what we are doing — and why it matters.

Together, we will rebuild trust, uphold gold-standard science, reverse chronic disease, and Make America Healthy Again.

With gratitude,

HHS Secretary Robert F. Kennedy, Jr.

Take Back Your Health Tour
 
 

Camelback Recovery in Peoria, AZ welcomed Secretary Kennedy on a tour of the facility’s residential treatment campus for its programs to bring individuals struggling with addiction onto the path of sustained recovery. They recorded an episode of the “I Love Being Sober” podcast celebrate living substance-free.

Secretary Kennedy visited Ability 360 in Phoenix where he met with leaders and community members working to support independent living for individuals with disabilities. The center highlighted unique programs focused on employment, mobility, and adaptive fitness.

In Scottsdale, Secretary Kennedy met with Banner Health leadership to see firsthand how innovation is expanding access to community-based care, from pediatric services to life-saving breast cancer screenings.

At the Marana Health Center, Secretary Kennedy joined Rep. Juan Ciscomani for a roundtable highlighting preventive care in rural communities. HHS announced new investment opportunities to expand rural physician training and deliver $125 million to scale nutrition services nationwide.

At the Tribal Self Governance Conference, Secretary Kennedy highlighted how HHS incorporated traditional foods into the Dietary Guidelines for Americans and invested $1 billion to eliminate the IHS infrastructure backlog.
 
Division Spotlight: ACF
 
 

To deliver on President Trump and the First Lady’s Fostering the Future Executive Order, the HHS Administration for Children and Families (ACF) launched “A Home for Every Child,” a nationwide push to improve the ratio of foster homes to children in foster care across the United States.

Right now, nearly two children enter foster care for every one licensed home. ACF is working to change that.

Thanks to the leadership of ACF Assistant Secretary Alex Adams, 14 states and the District of Columbia have joined the program in its first 100 days. ACF is working to welcome even more states to join in this important work and close the gap between children in need and available homes.
